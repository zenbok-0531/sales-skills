The Methodology Stack
Framework Rubric Stack
Dunford vs. MEDDIC, Challenger, Sandler

It's not one of four. It's the foundation under the other three.

Most organizations try to pick a sales methodology. That's the mistake. Dunford-style positioning isn't a peer to MEDDIC, Challenger, or Sandler — it sits underneath them. When positioning is weak, every downstream methodology becomes generic. When it's strong, they all become sharper.

April = what story you tell. Challenger = how you deliver it. Sandler = how you manage the conversation. MEDDIC = whether the deal holds up.
01

Four methodologies, four jobs.

Each solves a different layer of the problem. Knowing which layer is which keeps you from confusing a positioning problem for a qualification problem — a mistake that wastes coaching cycles.

Layer 01 · Story

Dunford

Define how the product should be understood. Frame the problem, category, and differentiation so buyers get it quickly and correctly.

Layer 02 · Delivery

Challenger

Change how the buyer thinks about their problem. Teach something non-obvious, create constructive tension.

Layer 03 · Control

Sandler

Control the interaction and qualification process. Set boundaries, disqualify ruthlessly, don't chase.

Layer 04 · Close

MEDDIC

Validate whether the deal is real and winnable. Know where it stands, anticipate risk, build internal alignment.

03

What changes with Dunford in place.

The easiest way to see the foundation effect is side-by-side. Same methodology, same rep, same conversation — but with and without strong positioning underneath.

Dunfordvs.Challenger

Dunford provides the content. Challenger provides the delivery. Both are required; they are not substitutes.

Dunford says

Here's what you should teach them.

Positioning defines the specific, differentiated insight worth delivering.

Challenger says

Teach the customer something new.

The method is sound. But it's a vessel — it needs content to carry.

Without Dunford
"AI is changing everything in crisis management…"
With Dunford
"You're not struggling with alerts — you're struggling with decision-making in the messy middle."
Dunford = content. Challenger = delivery. Without the first, the second sounds like every other vendor.
Dunfordvs.Sandler

Dunford sharpens Sandler's discovery depth by defining what "pain" actually means in the category.

Dunford defines

What pain actually means in your category, why it matters now, and what alternative approaches exist.

Sandler focuses on

Pain, budget, decision. Without a sharpened definition of pain, Sandler discovery stays at surface level.

Without Dunford
Pain = "too many alerts"
With Dunford
Pain = "making decisions without verified context, creating risk exposure at the executive level."
Dunford sharpens Sandler's discovery depth. Pain is only useful if it's specific enough to act on.
Dunfordvs.MEDDIC

Dunford gives MEDDIC the narrative that makes metrics believable and the context for why you win.

Dunford provides

The value narrative that makes metrics believable. The context for why you win vs. named alternatives.

MEDDIC needs

Metrics, economic impact, clear differentiation. Without a narrative, the metrics read as generic ROI math.

Without Dunford
"Save time on alert triage."
With Dunford
"Reduce time-to-decision under uncertainty. Avoid escalation risk before full information is available."
Dunford gives MEDDIC teeth. Metrics without a narrative are generic. Metrics inside a narrative are decisive.
04

The skills each methodology demands.

Four different core skills, four different coaching arcs. A team that's strong on one methodology isn't automatically strong on another — they require distinct capabilities built through distinct reps.

Layer 01 · Story

Dunford

Core skill: Strategic narrative thinking
  • Problem framingTurning vague pain into clear, named problems.
  • Category creation"This isn't alerting vs. alerting — it's alerting vs. decision platforms."
  • DifferentiationExplaining why you're different in a way that matters.
  • Story compressionComplex value articulated in 30–60 seconds.
Rare skill. Most reps struggle here.
Layer 02 · Delivery

Challenger

Core skill: Insight delivery with authority
  • Commercial insightBringing non-obvious, valuable perspective.
  • ReframingShifting how the buyer sees their problem.
  • Constructive tensionChallenging assumptions without abrasion.
  • Executive communicationSpeaking in outcomes, not features.
Hard skill — requires confidence and credibility.
Layer 03 · Control

Sandler

Core skill: Conversational control and qualification
  • Deep questioningGetting beyond surface pain.
  • Active listeningPicking up nuance and signals.
  • Boundary settingNot over-serving or over-selling.
  • DisqualificationWalking away from bad deals.
Hard skill — requires emotional discipline.
Layer 04 · Close

MEDDIC

Core skill: Deal engineering and navigation
  • Stakeholder mappingIdentifying power vs. influence.
  • Champion buildingTurning users into internal sellers.
  • Process navigationProcurement and approvals fluency.
  • Value quantificationTranslating solution into business impact.
Hard skill — requires organizational intelligence.
05

What great looks like, combined.

A top-tier enterprise rep runs all four methodologies in a single conversation — usually without naming them. Each comes out of a different moment, in a different voice, doing different work.

DunfordTo frame
"You don't have an alerting problem. You have a decision problem."
ChallengerTo push
"Most teams don't realize how much time they lose in the investigation gap."
SandlerTo qualify
"If this isn't a priority this quarter, we probably shouldn't continue."
MEDDICTo close
"Who ultimately signs off — and how do we align this to their priorities?"
The samdesk Read

Strong in Dunford. Upside in Challenger and MEDDIC.

Your team is already strong in April-style thinking. The positioning instinct is there — decision platform vs. alerting, verified context vs. raw signal, the strategic frame lands when reps deploy it.

The biggest upside is operationalizing that positioning through Challenger + MEDDIC in the field. Reps have the story. What varies is whether they deliver it with authority (Challenger) and whether it translates into a deal structure that holds up under procurement pressure (MEDDIC).
